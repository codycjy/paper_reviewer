      "summary_comment": "This paper presents a novel approach to address the memory-convergence trade-off in fine-tuning large language models on resource-constrained devices. By integrating Block Coordinate Descent (BCD) with Hessian-informed zeroth-order optimization, the authors propose B-PDF, a method that significantly reduces memory overhead while maintaining competitive accuracy and convergence speed. The idea of selectively updating layers and their corresponding diagonal Hessian information is quite innovative and directly tackles a practical problem. While the theoretical analysis heavily relies on prior work and the evaluation on the largest model (LLaMA-2-7B) is somewhat preliminary, the primary contribution of a truly memory-efficient, second-order capable fine-tuning method is highly significant. The potential for this work to make LLM fine-tuning more accessible on lower-end hardware aligns perfectly with pushing the field forward. Minor imperfections in theoretical depth or exhaustive evaluation are acceptable given the strong novelty and practical impact."
