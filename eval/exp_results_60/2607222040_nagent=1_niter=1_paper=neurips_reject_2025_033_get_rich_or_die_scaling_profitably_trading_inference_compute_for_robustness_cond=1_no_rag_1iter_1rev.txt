This idea challenges conventional wisdom and provides a compelling framework for understanding why reasoning-based defenses might fail against certain adversarial attacks and succeed against others. The authors develop creative experimental setups, including a novel white-box attack using textual repetition, to validate their hypothesis across models with varying levels of robustness. The finding that robust models benefit disproportionately more from increased inference-time compute, leading to a 'rich-get-richer' dynamic, is a crucial insight. While there are some minor methodological heterogeneities in how 'inference-time compute' is scaled across experiments, and the scale of the models used is a recognized limitation, the bold idea and its potential to guide future research in a new direction make this paper highly valuable. The creativity in testing the hypothesis with current resources is commendable.
