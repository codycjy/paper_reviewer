# Review

## Summary
The paper studies the asymptotic properties of the expected signature estimator. The main contribution is a set of conditions under which the expected signature estimator displays important asymptotic statistical properties, namely consistency and asymptotic normality. The authors also devise a modification of the expected signature estimator with better finite sample properties when the latent data generating process is a martingale.

## Strengths
The paper is well-written and clear. The authors provide a comprehensive set of conditions under which the expected signature estimator exhibits desirable asymptotic properties. The theoretical results are well-explained and the proof sketch is clear. The authors also provide a novel variance reduction technique for the expected signature estimator.

## Weaknesses
1. The paper does not provide any experimental results to validate the theoretical findings. The authors could provide some experimental results to demonstrate the practical performance of the modified estimator and compare it with the original estimator.

2. The paper does not provide any guidance on how to choose the sequence of partitions used to approximate the continuous-time stochastic process. The authors could provide some recommendations on how to select the sequence of partitions based on the desired accuracy and computational efficiency.
