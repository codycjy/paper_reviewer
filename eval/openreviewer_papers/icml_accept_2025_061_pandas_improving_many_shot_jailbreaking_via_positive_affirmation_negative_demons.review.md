# Review

## Summary
This paper introduces PANDAS, a hybrid technique that enhances many-shot jailbreaking by incorporating positive affirmations, negative demonstrations, and adaptive sampling. The authors also present ManyHarm, a dataset of harmful question-answer pairs, and demonstrate through extensive experiments that PANDAS significantly outperforms baseline methods in long-context scenarios.

## Strengths
1. The paper is well-organized and easy to follow.
2. The authors present a new dataset called ManyHarm, which consists of 2,400 malicious demonstrations spanning 12 types of harmful behaviors.
3. The authors conduct extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of PANDAS in improving jailbreaking success rates over existing methods.

## Weaknesses
1. The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency of PANDAS, which could be an important consideration for practical applications.
2. The paper does not provide a comprehensive discussion of the potential defenses against PANDAS or other jailbreaking attacks.
3. The authors do not explore the potential ethical implications of the use of PANDAS or other jailbreaking techniques, such as the potential for harm to users or the broader impact of the attack.

## Questions
1. How does the computational efficiency of PANDAS compare to other jailbreaking methods, particularly in terms of the resources required for the attack?
2. Are there any potential defenses against PANDAS that the authors considered or evaluated? What are the authors' thoughts on the feasibility of defending against this attack?
3. What are the potential ethical implications of the use of PANDAS or other jailbreaking techniques? How can we mitigate any harm to users or the broader impact of the attack?
