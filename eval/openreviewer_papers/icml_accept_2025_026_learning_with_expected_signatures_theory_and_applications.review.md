# Review

## Summary
The paper presents a comprehensive theoretical framework for expected signature estimation, bridging the gap between continuous-time theory and discrete-time empirical estimators. It introduces a novel variance reduction technique using martingale correction, which is shown to significantly improve finite sample properties. The authors provide rigorous mathematical proofs and establish asymptotic normality of the estimator under various conditions. They also present practical examples and experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of their methods in real-world applications, including time series classification, pricing path-dependent derivatives, and distributional regression for streams. The paper contributes to the field by offering a more complete probabilistic interpretation of expected signature-based methods and by advancing the practical performance of these techniques through the martingale correction approach.

## Strengths
The paper is clearly written and well-organized. The authors effectively communicate complex mathematical concepts and ideas through clear language and structured presentation. The proofs and theoretical results are presented in a step-by-step manner, which helps readers understand the logical flow and reasoning behind the findings. ## Weaknesses

While the authors provide clear and concise explanations of the theoretical framework, a more detailed discussion of the assumptions and their limitations would enhance the understanding of the scope and applicability of the proposed methods. Additionally, the paper could include more detailed explanations of the experimental results, including discussions of the strengths and limitations of the proposed methods in practical applications.
