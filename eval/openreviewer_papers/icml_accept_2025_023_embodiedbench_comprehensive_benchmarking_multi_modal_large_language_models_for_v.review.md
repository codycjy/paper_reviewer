# Review

## Summary
The paper presents a new benchmark for evaluating vision-driven embodied agents. The benchmark contains a diverse set of 1,128 testing tasks across 4 environments. The tasks cover a range of difficulty levels, from high-level semantic tasks to low-level tasks requiring atomic actions. It also evaluates six key agent capabilities: commonsense reasoning, complex instruction understanding, spatial awareness, visual perception, and long-term planning. The authors evaluated 24 leading proprietary and open-source MLLMs within the benchmark. The results show that while MLLMs perform well on high-level tasks, they struggle with low-level manipulation tasks.

## Strengths
1. The benchmark covers a wide range of tasks and environments, providing a comprehensive evaluation of agent capabilities.
2. The benchmark highlights the challenges faced by current MLLMs in low-level manipulation tasks, which is an area for potential improvement.
3. The paper provides valuable insights into the performance of different MLLMs and offers suggestions for future research directions.

## Weaknesses
1. The paper does not provide a clear comparison with existing benchmarks, making it difficult to assess the novelty and significance of the proposed benchmark.
2. The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the error cases, which could provide more insights into the limitations of current MLLMs.

## Questions
1. How does the proposed benchmark compare to existing ones in terms of task diversity and complexity?
2. What are the main insights from the error analysis, and how do they align with the broader research landscape?
