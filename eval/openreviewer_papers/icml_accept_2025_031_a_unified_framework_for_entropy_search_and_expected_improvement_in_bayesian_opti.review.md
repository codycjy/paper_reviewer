# Review

## Summary
This paper introduces a novel acquisition function for Bayesian optimization. It is based on a variational lower bound of an information-theoretic acquisition function called MES. The proposed acquisition function can be seen as a generalization of EI, which is a special case of MES. The authors show that the proposed acquisition function performs competitively with EI and MES on synthetic and real-world problems.

## Strengths
- The proposed acquisition function is well-motivated.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- The proposed acquisition function is novel and performs competitively with EI and MES on synthetic and real-world problems.

## Weaknesses
- The proposed acquisition function is computationally more expensive than EI and MES.
- The performance of the proposed acquisition function is similar to that of EI on some problems.
- The interpretation of the proposed acquisition function is unclear.

## Questions
- The authors mention that the proposed acquisition function is computationally intensive. Can they provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost? How does the computational cost compare to that of EI and MES?
- The authors mention that it is unclear if there is a clear and straightforward interpretation of the proposed acquisition function. Can they provide any insights or hypotheses about the interpretation of the proposed acquisition function?
