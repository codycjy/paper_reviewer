# Review

## Summary
This paper proposes KARPA, a novel framework that leverages the global planning and reasoning capabilities of LLMs to enhance KGQA tasks. The key innovation is using an LLM to pre-plan logically coherent relation paths, followed by a semantic embedding model to retrieve candidate paths that are then used for comprehensive reasoning with the LLM. Extensive experiments demonstrate state-of-the-art performance across multiple datasets and evaluation metrics while being training-free and compatible with various LLM architectures.

## Strengths
- The paper is well-written, with clear motivation and illustrations. The proposed method is novel and well-explained.
- The training-free approach is particularly valuable as it enables seamless integration with various LLMs, providing a plug-and-play solution that enhances their reasoning capabilities over KGs without the need for extensive retraining or fine-tuning.
- The proposed method significantly reduces the number of LLM-KG interactions, which not only enhances efficiency but also lowers computational costs compared to previous methods like ToG.

## Weaknesses
- The evaluation is primarily conducted on WebQSP and CWQ, which may not fully capture the diversity of real-world KGs. It would be beneficial to include more datasets with different characteristics to validate the generalizability of KARPA.
- While the paper demonstrates strong performance with GPT-4 and other LLMs, it's unclear how KARPA performs with smaller or more resource-constrained LLMs. Additional experiments with a wider range of LLMs, particularly smaller ones, would strengthen the claims of universality.
- The paper lacks a thorough analysis of the computational efficiency and scalability of KARPA. It would be valuable to provide detailed metrics on inference time, memory usage, and scalability tests with larger KGs to understand the practical deployment requirements.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed error analysis to understand the failure modes of KARPA. This would provide insights into the limitations of the approach and potential areas for improvement.
- The method relies on the quality of the semantic embedding model used for relation path retrieval. An analysis of the impact of different embedding models on performance would strengthen the paper.

## Questions
- Have you considered any strategies to reduce the length of the reasoning paths generated by KARPA? Longer paths may lead to increased computational complexity and inference time.
- How does KARPA handle ambiguous or underspecified questions where multiple reasoning paths might be equally valid? Is there a mechanism to resolve such cases?
- Have you explored the potential of integrating KARPA with other KGQA methods, such as training-based approaches, to further enhance performance?
