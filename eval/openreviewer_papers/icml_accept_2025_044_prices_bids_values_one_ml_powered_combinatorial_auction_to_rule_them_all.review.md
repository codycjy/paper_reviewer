# Review

## Summary
The paper studies iterative combinatorial auctions (ICAs) and the tradeoffs between value and demand queries. The authors introduce a new algorithm that leverages both query types and demonstrate its superior performance compared to existing methods through experiments.

## Strengths
The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors present a natural and intuitive algorithm, and the experimental results effectively demonstrate its superiority. Additionally, the authors provide a thorough literature review and discuss many interesting aspects of ICAs.

## Weaknesses
I have two major concerns about the paper.

1. The paper does not provide any guarantee for the final efficiency of the algorithm. The efficiency achieved in the experiments appears to be highly dependent on the specific dataset used. It would be beneficial to understand how the algorithm's performance varies with different datasets and to have some provable guarantees.

2. The paper lacks a discussion on the computational complexity of the algorithm. It would be helpful to know the time required to run the algorithm in the experiments and to understand how the computational cost scales with the number of participants and items.
