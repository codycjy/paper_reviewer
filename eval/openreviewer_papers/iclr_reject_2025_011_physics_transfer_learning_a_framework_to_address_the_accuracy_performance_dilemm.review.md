# Review

## Summary
The paper proposes a physics-transfer (PT) learning framework to learn physics across digital models of varying fidelities and complexities. The capability of the approach is showcased through screening metallic alloys by their strengths and predicting the morphological development of brains.

## Strengths
1. The paper proposes a physics-transfer (PT) learning framework to learn physics across digital models of varying fidelities and complexities, which addresses the accuracy-performance dilemma in understanding representative multiscale problems.

2. The proposed method is validated on two representative cases, including materials strength screening and predicting the development of brain morphologies.

## Weaknesses
1. The proposed PT framework is not well-defined. The authors should clarify how the PT framework learns physics across digital models of varying fidelities and complexities, and how it addresses the accuracy-performance dilemma. More details and explanations are needed.

2. The paper lacks a clear definition of the problem and a description of the corresponding dataset. The authors should provide more details about the problem they are trying to solve and the dataset they are using. This would help readers better understand the context and significance of the work.

3. The experimental section of the paper is not well-developed. The authors should provide more details about the experiments, including the data used, the specific tasks performed, and the results obtained. Additionally, the authors should discuss the limitations of their approach and potential areas for future research.

## Questions
1. What are the limitations of the proposed PT framework? The authors should discuss the limitations of their approach and potential areas for future research.

2. How does the PT framework compare to other state-of-the-art methods for learning physics across digital models of varying fidelities and complexities? The authors should compare their approach to other relevant methods and discuss the advantages and disadvantages of their approach in comparison to these methods.
