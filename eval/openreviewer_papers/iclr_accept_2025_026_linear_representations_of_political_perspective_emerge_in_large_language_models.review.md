# Review

## Summary
This paper investigates whether LLMs possess linear representations of political perspectives. The authors prompt LLMs to generate text from the perspectives of different U.S. lawmakers and train linear probes to predict their DW-NOMINATE scores based on the activations of the models' attention heads. The results show that LLMs possess a linear representation of the "liberal–conservative" political axis in American politics.

## Strengths
- The paper is well-structured and easy to follow. 
- The experiments are well-executed and provide convincing evidence of linear representations of political perspectives in LLMs.

## Weaknesses
- The study focuses solely on the "liberal–conservative" political axis in American politics, which may limit the generalizability of the findings to other political systems or perspectives not covered.
- While the paper demonstrates the existence of linear representations, it does not explore the underlying reasons or mechanisms that give rise to these representations. A more in-depth analysis of why these representations emerge in LLMs could provide valuable insights into the models' behavior and potentially inform ways to mitigate any biases.

## Questions
- Could the authors elaborate on how their findings might generalize to other political systems or perspectives not covered in the study?
- What factors do the authors think contribute to the emergence of linear representations of political perspectives in LLMs? How might these factors influence the strength or weakness of such representations in different models?
