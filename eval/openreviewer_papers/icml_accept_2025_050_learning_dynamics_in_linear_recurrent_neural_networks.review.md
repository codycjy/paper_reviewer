# Review

## Summary
The paper studies the learning dynamics of linear RNNs (LRNNs) and how they are affected by the temporal structure of the data. The authors derive an analytical solution to the energy function and learning dynamics of LRNNs under certain conditions. They find that the learning of data singular values is ordered by both scale and temporal precedence, with larger and later singular values being learned faster. They also identify how task dynamics impact solution stability and extrapolation ability, and describe a tradeoff between recurrent and feedforward computation that leads to low-rank solutions. Furthermore, the authors derive the neural tangent kernel for finite-width LRNNs and show that recurrence encourages feature learning. Finally, they demonstrate the applicability of their results in a sensory integration task where they relax their prior assumptions.

## Strengths
1. The paper provides a novel framework for studying the learning dynamics of LRNNs and the effect of task dynamics. This framework allows for a more comprehensive understanding of the learning process in LRNNs and how it is affected by the temporal structure of the data.

2. The authors derive an analytical solution to the energy function and learning dynamics of LRNNs under certain conditions. This solution reveals the ordering of the learning of data singular values and how it is affected by both scale and temporal precedence.

3. The paper identifies how task dynamics impact solution stability and extrapolation ability. This is an important contribution as it sheds light on how the properties of the task dynamics can affect the learning process and the resulting solutions.

4. The authors describe a tradeoff between recurrent and feedforward computation that leads to low-rank solutions. This finding is significant as it explains the emergence of low-rank connectivity in LRNNs and how it is mediated by an effective regularization term in the energy function.

5. The paper derives the neural tangent kernel for finite-width LRNNs and shows that recurrence encourages feature learning. This is a valuable contribution as it provides insights into how the recurrence can affect the learning process and the representational capabilities of LRNNs.

6. The authors demonstrate the applicability of their results in a sensory integration task where they relax their prior assumptions. This demonstrates the generality of their framework and the robustness of their findings.

## Weaknesses
1. The paper assumes that the input-output correlation matrices have constant left and right singular vectors across trajectory timesteps. Although this assumption holds for data generated by a teacher linear RNN with weights that can be diagonalized, it may not hold for more general data generating processes. This limitation could affect the applicability of the results to real-world tasks.

2. The paper focuses on a specific class of LRNNs with data-aligned weights and tasks with input-output correlations that have constant singular/eigen-vectors. While this setting allows for analytical results, it may not capture the full range of possible LRNN architectures and tasks. This limitation could affect the generalizability of the findings to other LRNNs and tasks.

3. The paper assumes that the LRNN is data-aligned at initialization. While Atanasov et al. (2022) showed that this alignment happens early in training for networks initialized with small random weights, it may not hold for other initializations. This limitation could affect the applicability of the results to LRNNs with different initialization schemes.

4. The paper does not provide experimental results to validate the theoretical findings. While the analytical results are insightful, experimental results would provide empirical evidence for the proposed mechanisms and algorithms. This limitation could affect the credibility and practical significance of the work.

## Questions
1. How does the assumption of constant singular vectors affect the applicability of the results? Are there any practical scenarios where this assumption is realistic?

2. How does the assumption of data-aligned weights at initialization affect the applicability of the results? Are there any practical scenarios where this assumption is realistic?

3. How does the proposed framework compare to existing approaches for studying the learning dynamics of RNNs? Are there any advantages or disadvantages compared to other frameworks?

4. How does the proposed algorithm compare to existing approaches for training LRNNs? Are there any advantages or disadvantages compared to other algorithms?

5. Are there any practical applications or implications of the proposed framework and algorithm? How can they be used to improve the performance of LRNNs in real-world tasks?
