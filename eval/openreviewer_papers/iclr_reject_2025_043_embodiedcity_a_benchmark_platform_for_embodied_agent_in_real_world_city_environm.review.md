# Review

## Summary
The paper presents a new benchmark for embodied agents in real-world urban environments. The benchmark includes a highly realistic 3D simulation of a city district, dynamic pedestrian and vehicle traffic, and a set of evaluation tasks covering scene understanding, question answering, dialogue, visual language navigation, and task planning. The authors evaluate several large language models on these tasks and provide a Python SDK for easy access to the simulator and benchmark.

## Strengths
1. The paper introduces a new benchmark for embodied agents in real-world urban environments, addressing an important gap in existing research. The benchmark includes a highly realistic 3D simulation of a city district, dynamic pedestrian and vehicle traffic, and a set of evaluation tasks covering scene understanding, question answering, dialogue, visual language navigation, and task planning. The tasks are well-designed to assess different aspects of embodied intelligence, and the use of real-world data enhances the relevance and applicability of the benchmark.

2. The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of several large language models on the proposed benchmark, offering valuable insights into their performance and limitations. The evaluation covers multiple tasks and metrics, allowing for a detailed comparison of the models' capabilities.

3. The authors provide a Python SDK for easy access to the simulator and benchmark, allowing researchers to quickly and easily evaluate their models. This SDK is a valuable resource for the community and facilitates further research and development in this area.

## Weaknesses
1. The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing benchmarks for embodied agents, making it difficult to assess the novelty and significance of the proposed benchmark. While the authors mention some existing benchmarks in the introduction, a more thorough comparison in terms of scope, realism, and tasks would strengthen the paper.

2. The paper lacks a clear definition of the target audience for the benchmark. Is it intended for researchers in robotics, autonomous driving, or another field? Understanding the target audience would help in tailoring the benchmark to their specific needs and interests.

3. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the data collection process, including how the 3D models of buildings and streets were obtained, how the pedestrian and vehicle traffic data was collected, and how the evaluation tasks were designed and implemented. This lack of detail makes it difficult to assess the quality and reliability of the benchmark.

4. The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the performance of the evaluated models on different aspects of the benchmark, such as scene understanding versus task planning. A more in-depth analysis would provide valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of the models and help identify areas for improvement.

5. The paper does not provide a clear roadmap for future work, outlining potential extensions to the benchmark or applications of the results. A discussion of potential extensions, such as incorporating more complex tasks or integrating with real-world data, would help in identifying directions for future research.

This would help in assessing the quality and reliability of your benchmark.

4. Can you provide a more in-depth analysis of the performance of the evaluated models on different aspects of the benchmark, such as scene understanding versus task planning? This would help in identifying areas for improvement and understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the models.

5. What are your plans for future work? Please provide a clear roadmap for extending the benchmark or applying the results to real-world problems. This would help in identifying directions for future research and demonstrating the practical impact of your work.
