# Review

## Summary
This paper introduces a new problem: Best Interval Approximation. The goal is to assign intervals to the vertices of a signed graph such that the positive edges are covered by overlapping intervals and the negative edges are covered by non-overlapping intervals. The authors show that this problem is more expressive than the well-studied correlation clustering problem. They also show that the problem is NP-hard and provide a PTAS for complete graphs when the number of intervals k is fixed. Finally, they propose heuristic algorithms and conduct experiments to evaluate their performance.

## Strengths
1. The authors propose a new and more expressive problem than the well-studied correlation clustering problem.
2. The authors show that the proposed problem is NP-hard and provide a PTAS for complete graphs when the number of intervals k is fixed.
3. The authors conduct extensive experiments to evaluate the performance of their proposed heuristic algorithms.
4. The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

## Weaknesses
1. The motivation of the proposed problem is not very clear. The authors should provide more examples to illustrate the importance of the proposed problem.
2. The proposed heuristic algorithms are not very efficient. The authors should provide a more detailed analysis of the time complexity of their proposed algorithms.
3. The authors should provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results. For example, they should explain why their proposed algorithms outperform the correlation clustering algorithms.
