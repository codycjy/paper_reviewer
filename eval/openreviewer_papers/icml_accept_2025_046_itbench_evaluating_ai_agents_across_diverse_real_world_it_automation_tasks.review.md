# Review

## Summary
The paper introduces ITBench, a benchmarking framework designed to evaluate AI agents on real-world IT automation tasks across three key areas: Site Reliability Engineering (SRE), Compliance and Security Operations (CISO), and Financial Operations (FinOps). ITBench provides a systematic methodology for assessing AI agent performance on 102 scenarios, offering an initial set of scenarios, tools, and leaderboard to drive innovation in AI-driven IT automation. The paper reports that state-of-the-art models resolve only 11.4% of SRE scenarios, 25.2% of CISO scenarios, and 25.8% of FinOps scenarios, highlighting the challenges and opportunities for AI agents in IT automation.

## Strengths
1. The paper introduces ITBench, a novel benchmarking framework that addresses a critical need for evaluating AI agents on real-world IT automation tasks. This is a significant contribution to the field, as it provides a systematic methodology for assessing AI agent performance in practical scenarios.

2. The framework is comprehensive, covering three key areas of IT automation: SRE, CISO, and FinOps. This wide scope ensures that AI researchers and practitioners can evaluate their agents across a diverse set of tasks, making the framework more reflective of real-world IT challenges.

3. ITBench includes an initial set of 102 scenarios, along with tools, APIs, and a leaderboard, which are essential for driving innovation and competition in the field. The framework's open-source nature and extensibility to new scenarios and personas demonstrate its potential to become a widely adopted standard for evaluating AI agents in IT automation.

## Weaknesses
1. The paper mentions that the initial release of ITBench includes only 102 scenarios, which may not be sufficient to fully capture the complexity and diversity of real-world IT systems. While the authors plan to extend the framework, the limited number of scenarios in the initial release could restrict the scope of the evaluation.

2. The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with existing benchmarks or frameworks for evaluating AI agents. It would be beneficial to discuss how ITBench differentiates itself from other benchmarks and what specific advantages it offers in terms of realism, comprehensiveness, and usability.

3. The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the performance of different AI models on ITBench. While it reports the overall success rates, a deeper dive into the results, including failure modes and reasons, would provide valuable insights for researchers and practitioners.

## Questions
1. How does ITBench differentiate itself from other existing benchmarks for evaluating AI agents, particularly in terms of realism and comprehensiveness? A detailed comparison would help establish its unique value proposition.

2. Can the authors provide more insights into the failure modes and reasons observed during the evaluation of different AI models on ITBench? This would be valuable for researchers and practitioners looking to understand the challenges and opportunities in AI-driven IT automation.

3. How does ITBench plan to address the potential limitations of the initial set of 102 scenarios in capturing the full spectrum of real-world IT automation tasks? Are there plans to extend the framework with more scenarios and use cases in the future?
