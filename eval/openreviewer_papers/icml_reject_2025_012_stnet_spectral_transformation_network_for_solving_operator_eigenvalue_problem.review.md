# Review

## Summary
This paper proposes a new method for solving eigenvalue problems. The method is based on the power method and uses neural networks to represent the eigenvectors. Deflation and filtering techniques are used to improve the convergence of the method. Numerical experiments are performed to demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.

## Strengths
The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The proposed method is a reasonable extension of the classical power method. The numerical experiments show that the method can achieve high accuracy for the problems considered.

## Weaknesses
1. The proposed method is an iterative method and the convergence of the method is not analyzed in the paper. The authors should provide some theoretical analysis of the convergence rate of the method.
2. The authors only perform numerical experiments on a few simple model problems. It is not clear how the method performs for more challenging problems, such as the ones encountered in real-world applications.
3. The authors do not compare their method with other state-of-the-art methods for solving eigenvalue problems, such as the ones based on the Arnoldi iteration and the subspace iteration method. It is difficult to evaluate the effectiveness of the method without such comparisons.
