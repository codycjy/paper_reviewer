# Review

## Summary
This paper studies ensembles of overparameterized random feature (RF) models. The authors first prove that, under a subexponential assumption, an infinite ensemble of finite-width ridgeless RF models is pointwise equivalent to a single infinite-width ridgeless RF model. This result implies that the generalization performance of an ensemble of overparameterized models is similar to that of a single large overparameterized model. The authors further show that this equivalence holds approximately in the small ridge regime. They also conduct experiments to verify their theoretical results.

## Strengths
This paper is well-written and easy to follow. The theoretical results are solid and novel, and the experiments are well-designed and clearly presented. I believe this paper makes a valuable contribution to the theoretical understanding of overparameterized ensembles.

## Weaknesses
The subexponential assumption in this paper is more general than the Gaussian assumption. However, in high-dimensional regimes, these two assumptions are essentially equivalent. I am curious whether the authors have any thoughts on whether the main results could be extended to the Gaussian setting.
