# Review

## Summary
This paper studies the problem of online uniform sampling, where the goal is to distribute a sampling budget uniformly across unknown decision times. The authors present the first randomized algorithm designed for this problem and subsequently extend it to incorporate learning augmentation. They provide worst-case approximation guarantees for both algorithms, and illustrate the utility of the algorithms through both synthetic experiments and a real-world case study involving the HeartSteps mobile application. The numerical results show strong empirical average performance of the proposed randomized algorithms against previously proposed heuristic solutions.

## Strengths
The paper introduces a novel problem, online uniform sampling (OUS), and presents the first randomized algorithm designed for this problem. It extends the algorithm to incorporate learning augmentation and provides worst-case approximation guarantees. The authors illustrate the utility of the algorithms through both synthetic experiments and a real-world case study involving the HeartSteps mobile application. The numerical results show strong empirical average performance of the proposed randomized algorithms against previously proposed heuristic solutions.

## Weaknesses
1. The paper is not well-written and it is not easy to follow. For example, the description of the problem is confusing and difficult to understand. In the abstract and introduction, the authors mention that the goal is to distribute a sampling budget uniformly across unknown decision times, but they do not explain what the sampling budget is or what the decision times are. It would be helpful to provide a clear definition of the problem and explain the motivation behind it.
2. The paper lacks a thorough review of related work. While the authors mention some existing studies on digital interventions and uniform sampling, they do not provide a comprehensive review of related work on online algorithms and learning-augmented algorithms. It would be helpful to include a more detailed discussion of related work and how the proposed algorithms compare to existing approaches.
3. The theoretical analysis in the paper is not well-explained. The authors provide worst-case approximation guarantees for the proposed algorithms, but they do not explain the intuition behind the analysis or the implications of the guarantees. It would be helpful to provide a more detailed discussion of the theoretical analysis and how it relates to the practical performance of the algorithms.
4. The experimental evaluation in the paper is limited. The authors only evaluate the proposed algorithms on a single dataset (the HeartSteps mobile application) and do not compare them to existing approaches. It would be helpful to include a more comprehensive evaluation of the algorithms on multiple datasets and compare them to existing approaches.

## Questions
1. Can you provide a clear definition of the online uniform sampling problem and explain the motivation behind it?
2. Can you include a more detailed discussion of related work on online algorithms and learning-augmented algorithms?
3. Can you provide a more detailed discussion of the theoretical analysis and how it relates to the practical performance of the algorithms?
4. Can you include a more comprehensive evaluation of the algorithms on multiple datasets and compare them to existing approaches?
