# Review

## Summary
This paper proposes a method for multivariate conformal prediction using optimal transport. The proposed method leverages the optimal transport theory to define a ranking for multivariate data, which can then be used for conformal prediction. The authors provide both theoretical analysis and empirical results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

## Strengths
1. The paper is well-structured and easy to follow.
2. The paper provides a novel method for multivariate conformal prediction using optimal transport, which is a creative combination of existing ideas.
3. The paper includes both theoretical analysis and empirical results, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.

## Weaknesses
1. The proposed method is only compared with two other methods, which may not be enough to fully demonstrate its advantages and limitations. More baselines should be included.
2. The paper does not provide a detailed discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed method, which could be a potential limitation for large-scale datasets.
3. The paper does not provide a detailed discussion of the assumptions made by the proposed method, such as the regularity of the data distribution, which could affect the performance of the method.
4. The paper does not provide a detailed discussion of the sensitivity of the proposed method to its hyperparameters, such as the weight parameter in the loss function.

## Questions
1. How does the proposed method perform on datasets with different characteristics, such as different dimensions, noise levels, and distributions?
2. How does the proposed method compare with other multivariate conformal prediction methods that are not based on optimal transport?
3. How does the proposed method perform on large-scale datasets, and what are the computational costs associated with it?
4. How sensitive is the proposed method to its hyperparameters, and how should these be chosen in practice?
