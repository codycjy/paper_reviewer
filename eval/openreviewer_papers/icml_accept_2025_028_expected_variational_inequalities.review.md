# Review

## Summary
This paper introduces expected variational inequalities (EVIs), a relaxation of the classical variational inequality (VI) concept. Unlike VIs, which are generally computationally intractable, EVIs can be solved in polynomial time under minimal assumptions. The paper establishes several properties of EVIs, showing that they can be efficiently computed under specific conditions and that they generalize concepts like correlated equilibria in games. The paper also explores connections to other solution concepts and discusses the tractability of EVIs in various settings.

## Weaknesses
