# Review

## Summary
This paper studies the multiclass linear classification problem in the presence of random classification noise (RCN). In this problem, the learner is given labeled examples $(x,y)$, where $x$ is drawn from an unknown distribution in $\mathbb{R}^d$ and the label $y$ is generated by a multiclass linear classifier corrupted with RCN. The goal is to output a hypothesis with a small 0-1 error. This paper gives super-polynomial statistical query (SQ) lower bounds for this problem. Specifically, for the case of three labels and constant separation, the paper provides a super-polynomial lower bound on the complexity of any SQ algorithm that achieves the optimal error. For a larger number of labels and smaller separation, the paper shows that it is SQ-hard even to achieve any constant factor approximation to the optimal loss or to beat the trivial hypothesis.
