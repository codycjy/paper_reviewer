# Review

## Summary
This paper studies the problem of learning k-parity given d-bit inputs. The authors consider a one-layer transformer architecture and demonstrate that with chain of thought reasoning, the transformer can learn k-parity in a polynomial number of steps.

## Strengths
- The problem studied in this paper is well-motivated. Understanding the capabilities of transformers and the role of chain of thought reasoning in enabling such capabilities is an important problem.
- The theoretical results are strong. The authors prove that gradient descent with chain of thought reasoning can learn k-parity in polynomial time, in contrast, gradient descent without chain of thought reasoning requires exponential time to learn k-parity.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

## Weaknesses
- The authors did not provide any experimental results to verify their theoretical findings.

## Questions
- Would it be possible to conduct some simple experiments to verify the theoretical results?
