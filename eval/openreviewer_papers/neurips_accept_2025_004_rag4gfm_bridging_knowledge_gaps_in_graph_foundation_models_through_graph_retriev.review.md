# Review

## Summary
This paper proposes RAG4GFM, a RAG framework for graph foundation models. The framework consists of three components: multi-level graph indexing, task-aware retrieval, and graph fusion enhancement. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of RAG4GFM on several graph tasks and datasets.

## Strengths
1. The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
2. The proposed framework is reasonable and effective.
3. The experiments are comprehensive.

## Weaknesses
1. The novelty of the proposed method is limited. The multi-level graph index and task-aware retriever are very similar to those proposed in [1]. The attention-based feature fusion mechanism is also similar to the one in [2].
2. The motivation of this paper is not clear. The authors argue that RAG can mitigate hallucinations, but they do not provide any evidence to support this claim in the context of graph foundation models.
3. The paper lacks a discussion of the limitations of the proposed method.

[1] He, Xiaoxin, et al. "G-retriever: Retrieval-augmented generation for textual graph understanding and question answering." Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems 37 (2024).

[2] Tang, Jiabin, et al. "Higpt: Heterogeneous graph language model." Proceedings of the 30th ACM SIGKDD Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining. 2024.
