# Review

## Summary
This paper proposes a graphlet/orbits based explainer for GNNs. The authors argue that graphlets/orbits are human-interpretable and widely used in various scientific fields. They decompose the GNN weights into orbit units to extract class-specific graph patterns at the model level and identify important subgraphs within individual data instances for prediction at the instance level. The authors conduct extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their method.

## Strengths

## Weaknesses
1. The authors claim that graphlet/orbits are human-interpretable, however, they do not provide any empirical evidence. 

2. The authors only consider 2-5 node graphlet/orbits, which is not convincing enough. 

3. The proposed method is only applicable to node classification tasks.
