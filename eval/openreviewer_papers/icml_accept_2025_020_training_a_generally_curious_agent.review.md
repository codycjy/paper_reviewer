# Review

## Summary
This paper presents a method for fine-tuning LLMs for better decision-making capabilities. The method, PAPRIKA, is trained on a variety of decision-making tasks and can generalize to unseen tasks. The paper also introduces a curriculum learning strategy to improve sample efficiency.

## Strengths
- The paper is well-written and well-organized, with clear explanations of the proposed method and experimental results.
- The proposed method, PAPRIKA, is novel and has the potential to improve the decision-making capabilities of LLMs, which is a critical area for AI research.
- The introduction of a curriculum learning strategy to improve sample efficiency is a practical and innovative approach.

## Weaknesses
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods for improving LLM decision-making capabilities.
- The generalizability of the proposed method to a wider range of tasks and environments is not fully explored in the experiments.

## Questions
- How does PAPRIKA compare to other methods for improving LLM decision-making capabilities in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the generalizability of PAPRIKA to completely new and more complex decision-making tasks?
- How does the curriculum learning strategy affect the learning dynamics and final performance of PAPRIKA? Are there any trade-offs between sample efficiency and the quality of the learned strategies?
