# Review

## Summary
This paper introduces a method called Contrastive Sparse Representation (CSR) for adaptive representation learning, which sparsifies pre-trained embeddings into a high-dimensional but selectively activated feature space. CSR combines sparse autoencoding and task-aware contrastive objectives, allowing flexible and cost-effective inference at various sparsity levels. Experiments on image, text, and multimodal benchmarks show that CSR outperforms MRL in accuracy and retrieval speed while reducing training time. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of sparse coding for adaptive representation learning in real-world applications.
