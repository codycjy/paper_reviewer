# Review

## Summary
This paper presents a comprehensive analysis of the identifiability of deep polynomial neural networks (PNNs). The authors prove that deep PNNs are generically identifiable if every 2-layer subnetwork is identifiable for a subset of its inputs. They also provide conditions under which PNNs are identifiable, including pyramidal networks and bottleneck networks. The paper uses techniques from algebraic geometry and tensor decompositions to prove their results.

## Strengths
- The paper provides a novel and comprehensive analysis of the identifiability of deep PNNs, which is an important problem in the theory of neural networks. The results are presented in a clear and concise manner, with detailed proofs and implications for specific architectures. The paper also discusses the connection between PNNs and low-rank tensor decompositions, which is a novel perspective.

- The paper makes significant contributions to the theory of neural networks by providing a localization theorem that shows the identifiability of deep PNNs is closely tied to the identifiability of their 2-layer subnetworks. The results also have practical implications for the design and analysis of deep learning models.

## Weaknesses
- The paper is quite technical and may be difficult for readers without a strong background in algebraic geometry and tensor decompositions. More intuitive explanations and examples could be provided to help readers understand the main results and their implications.

- The paper focuses on the identifiability of deep PNNs, but it does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the expressivity of these networks. More discussion on the relationship between identifiability and expressivity would be helpful.

- The paper does not provide any experimental results to validate the theoretical findings. Some experiments on real-world datasets could be added to demonstrate the practical implications of the main results.

## Questions
- Can the authors provide more intuitive explanations and examples to help readers understand the main results and their implications?

- How does the identifiability of deep PNNs relate to their expressivity? Can the authors provide more discussion on this topic?

- Can the authors provide experimental results on real-world datasets to demonstrate the practical implications of the main results?
