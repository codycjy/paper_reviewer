# Review

## Summary
This paper proposes a theory of creativity for convolutional diffusion models. By incorporating inductive biases of locality and equivariance, the proposed model can generate novel images that lie far from their training data. The proposed model can predict the output of trained convolutional diffusion models with high accuracy. The paper also reveals a locally consistent patch mosaic mechanism of creativity.

## Strengths
- The paper proposes a novel theory of creativity for convolutional diffusion models, which is a significant contribution to the field of generative models.
- The proposed model is fully analytic and can predict the output of trained convolutional diffusion models with high accuracy, demonstrating its practical utility.
- The paper provides a mechanistic interpretation of the creativity in convolutional diffusion models, which can help us better understand the working mechanism of generative models.

## Weaknesses
- The paper only focuses on convolutional diffusion models, which is a bit narrow in scope. It would be interesting to see an extension to other types of generative models.
- The paper could provide more insights into how to improve the creativity of generative models based on the proposed theory.

## Questions
- How can the proposed theory of creativity be extended to other types of generative models beyond convolutional diffusion models?
- What are some potential ways to improve the creativity of generative models based on the proposed theory?
- How well does the proposed model perform on more complex datasets, such as ImageNet?
