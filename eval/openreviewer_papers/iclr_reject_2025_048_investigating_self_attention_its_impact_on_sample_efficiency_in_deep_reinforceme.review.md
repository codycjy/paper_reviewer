# Review

## Summary
This paper investigates the impact of self-attention mechanisms on sample efficiency in deep reinforcement learning (DRL). It evaluates different self-attention module designs in the Arcade Learning Environment and finds that each design affects sample complexity differently. The study focuses on state representation and exploration to understand these effects, revealing subtle influences from self-attention on the learning process. The paper contributes to optimizing DRL architectures by providing insights into mechanisms that improve performance in data-limited scenarios.
