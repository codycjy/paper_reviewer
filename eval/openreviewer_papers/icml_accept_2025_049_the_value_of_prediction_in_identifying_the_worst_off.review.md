# Review

## Summary
This paper studies the welfare impacts of prediction in equity-driven contexts. The authors develop a framework to evaluate the relative value of prediction in identifying the worst-off by studying the prediction-access ratio (PAR). They further conduct a case study on identifying long-term unemployment to examine the welfare impacts of prediction.

## Strengths
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- The authors conduct a comprehensive theoretical analysis.
- The case study on identifying long-term unemployment is interesting and well-executed.

## Weaknesses
- The authors should provide more details on the dataset used in the case study, such as the sample size, feature variables, and target variable.
- The authors should provide more details on the modeling process, such as the training process, hyperparameter selection, and tuning process.
- The authors should provide more details on the results of the case study, such as the model performance and the prediction-access ratio at different stages.
- The authors should discuss the limitations of the proposed framework and the case study.

## Questions
- What are the limitations of the proposed framework? Can it be applied to other scenarios, or are there certain conditions that need to be met?
- What are the limitations of the case study? How can the findings be generalized to other contexts?
