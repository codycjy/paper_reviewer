# Review

## Summary
This paper presents a physics-informed neural network (PINN) approach to predict the temperature profile in a furnace. The method is based on the Hottel zone method, which divides the furnace into multiple zones and solves the energy balance equations to find the zone temperatures. The proposed approach uses the Hottel zone method to generate training data and then applies different neural network architectures with PINN regularization to predict the zone temperatures. The results show that the physics-based regularizers improve the accuracy of the predictions across different architectures.
