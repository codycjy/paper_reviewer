# Review

## Summary
The paper introduces BAXBENCH, a novel evaluation benchmark designed to assess the capabilities of large language models (LLMs) in generating secure and correct backend code for web and cloud applications. BAXBENCH consists of 392 tasks across 28 scenarios, 14 frameworks, and 6 programming languages, focusing on the generation of complete backend modules that are both functionally correct and secure.

## Strengths
- This paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- This paper proposes a novel benchmark that can evaluate the ability of LLMs to generate secure and correct backend code.
- This paper conducts a comprehensive evaluation of 11 state-of-the-art LLMs on the proposed benchmark.

## Weaknesses
- The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion on the reasons behind the observed limitations of current LLMs in generating secure and correct backend code. While the paper highlights the limitations, it does not delve deeply into the specific challenges or underlying issues that LLMs face in this context.
- The paper could provide more detailed insights into how the benchmark addresses the complexity of backend code generation. Specifically, it would be helpful to understand how the benchmark captures the nuances of real-world backend development, such as handling multiple dependencies, integrating different components, and managing the complexity of large-scale applications.
- The paper could further discuss the implications of its findings for the future development of LLMs. Specifically, it would be valuable to explore how the insights gained from this benchmark could guide the design and training of next-generation LLMs, particularly in terms of improving their ability to generate secure and correct code.
