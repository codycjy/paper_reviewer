# Review

## Summary
This paper presents a novel multi-agent debate framework for defending against jailbreak attacks on VLMs. The framework involves multiple LLM agents with different levels of access to the visual input, and the authors explore various debate strategies among the agents. The paper evaluates the framework on the MM-SafetyBench dataset and compares it to two baseline methods.

## Strengths
1. The paper introduces a unique approach to defending against jailbreak attacks by leveraging multi-agent debate, which is an innovative application of LLMs in the context of visual attack defenses.
2. The authors conduct comprehensive experiments on the MM-SafetyBench dataset and provide detailed analyses of the results, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.

## Weaknesses
1. The paper lacks a clear explanation of how the multi-agent debate framework fundamentally differs from and improves upon existing methods of defending against visual attacks.
2. The paper does not adequately address the scalability of the proposed method, particularly in terms of computational resources and the number of agents that can be effectively involved in the debate process.
3. The paper fails to provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research.

## Questions
1. How does the multi-agent debate framework fundamentally differ from and improve upon existing methods of defending against visual attacks?
2. What are the scalability limitations of the proposed method, and how do these limitations affect its practical applicability?
3. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future research?
