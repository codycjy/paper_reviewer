# Review

## Summary
This paper proposes a novel evaluation metric, CPD, for assessing the effectiveness of attribution methods in XAI for time series data. The authors also introduce an improved integrated gradients approach, TIMING, which leverages a combination of segments strategy to capture complex temporal dependencies. The paper demonstrates that TIMING outperforms existing methods in terms of attribution faithfulness, coherence, and efficiency.

## Strengths
1. The paper addresses an important problem in the field of XAI for time series data, namely the need for better evaluation metrics and more effective attribution methods.
2. The proposed evaluation metric, CPD, is novel and well-motivated, addressing the limitations of existing metrics. The segment-based masking strategy in TIMING is also innovative and tailored to the characteristics of time series data.
3. The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of TIMING, comparing it to a diverse set of baseline methods on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The results demonstrate the superior performance of TIMING in terms of attribution faithfulness, coherence, and efficiency.

## Weaknesses
1. The paper lacks a clear and detailed description of the implementation of TIMING, making it difficult to understand and replicate the method. The authors should provide more information on the specific steps involved in computing the attributions and the hyperparameter settings used in their experiments.
2. The paper does not discuss the scalability of TIMING to larger and more complex time series datasets. The authors should provide more information on the computational complexity of their method and its performance on larger datasets.
3. The paper does not provide any qualitative examples or case studies that illustrate the practical utility of the explanations generated by TIMING. The authors should include some examples that demonstrate how TIMING can help users understand and interact with the model in a real-world scenario.

## Questions
1. Can you provide more details on the implementation of TIMING, including the specific steps involved in computing the attributions and the hyperparameter settings used in your experiments?
2. How does TIMING scale to larger and more complex time series datasets? Can you provide more information on the computational complexity of your method and its performance on larger datasets?
3. Can you provide some qualitative examples or case studies that illustrate the practical utility of the explanations generated by TIMING? How can TIMING help users understand and interact with the model in a real-world scenario?
