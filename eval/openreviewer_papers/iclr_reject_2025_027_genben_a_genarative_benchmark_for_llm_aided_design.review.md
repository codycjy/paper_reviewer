# Review

## Summary
This paper proposes a new benchmark for LLM-aided design. The authors claim that the proposed benchmark is different from the existing ones as it has better verification coverage, diverse difficulty tiering, generative benchmark against data contamination, and enhanced evaluation metrics. The authors also conduct experiments using several LLMs to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed benchmark.

## Strengths
+ The authors introduce a new benchmark for LLM-aided design. The benchmark seems to have better verification coverage, diverse difficulty tiering, generative benchmark against data contamination, and enhanced evaluation metrics compared to the existing benchmarks.

## Weaknesses
- The authors should discuss more about the new challenges that the proposed benchmark wants to address. For example, the authors claim that the proposed benchmark has better verification coverage compared to the existing benchmarks. However, the authors do not discuss what are the limitations of the existing benchmarks in verification coverage and how the proposed one can address the problem. Without such a discussion, it is difficult to identify the significance of the proposed benchmark.

- The authors should discuss more about the new insights that the proposed benchmark can bring to the LLM-aided design community. For example, the authors can discuss that the proposed benchmark can be used to identify the LLMs’ ability to handle some specific challenges in hardware design, which cannot be identified by the existing benchmarks. Without such a discussion, it is difficult to identify the significance of the proposed benchmark.

- The authors should discuss more about the new techniques or methods that are used in the proposed benchmark. For example, the authors claim that the proposed benchmark employs perturbation strategies to defend against memorization. The authors should discuss more about the technique and its novelty.

- The authors should conduct more experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed benchmark. For example, the authors can conduct experiments to demonstrate that the proposed benchmark can identify the LLMs’ ability to handle some specific challenges in hardware design, which cannot be identified by the existing benchmarks. Without such experiments, it is difficult to identify the effectiveness of the proposed benchmark.
