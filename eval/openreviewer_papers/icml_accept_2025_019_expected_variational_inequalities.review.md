# Review

## Summary
The paper introduces a natural relaxation of the $\phi$-equilibrium, which is referred to as expected variational inequalities ($\phi$-EVI). It is shown that $\phi$-EVI can be solved in polynomial time under general (non-monotone) operators. Furthermore, the paper also shows that $\phi$-EVI captures the seminal notion of correlated equilibria and can be generalized to settings such as smooth games, and games with coupled constraints or non-concave utilities.

## Strengths
1. The paper is well-written and well-organized. The motivation is clear and the contributions are highlighted.
2. The paper provides solid theoretical analysis of the proposed notion, including existence and complexity results.
3. The paper also shows that the proposed notion can generalize to some existing settings.

## Weaknesses
1. The proposed notion is somewhat incremental, as it is a natural relaxation of the $\phi$-equilibrium.
2. The computational complexity of the proposed method is not analyzed, which is important in practice.

## Questions
1. What is the computational complexity of the proposed method? How does it compare with existing methods?
2. The paper shows that $\phi$-EVI can generalize to some existing settings. However, it is not clear how and why the proposed method can generalize to these settings.
